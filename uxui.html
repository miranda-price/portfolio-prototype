Visit Our Cities Website Information Architecture

Summer 2021

Final Product

This website was collaboratively made with two of my peers. For this website, I was the information architect responsible for all written content as well as the content's organization. The site was a combination of three websites about individual cities.

The homepage pictured, links to full site

Ontology

For the information architecture's ontology, I created the domain and triples that would dictate the site's content. I incorporated some of the categories from the three separate websites I was combining as well as some other categories that made sense for a travel site. Then I defined how the categories related to each other to guide the site's structure for the site's taxonomy.

The markdown document's domain and triple sections pictured right

Taxonomy

For the information architecture's taxonomy, I decided the HTML tag for each type of content and the document structure and outline for each subpage on the site. I began by organizing the information based on what would make sense for a travel site, with the most important travel information placed first. After I had the content outlined, I used HTML tags to demonstrate the information hierarchy for the site and to give the site a clearer structure.

The taxonomy for the article of each city subpage pictured right

Choreography

For the information architecture's choreography, I explained my reasoning for each decision I made regarding the information hierarcy for the travel site. Each element of the site must have a reason to exist on the site and a reason to be located where it is on a page. At its core, my reasoning was based on what information users for a travel site would want to know about a city, as well as how soon they would want to learn said information.
